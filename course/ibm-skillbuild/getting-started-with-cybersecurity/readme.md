## 📚 Courses Overview

### Course 1 — Cybersecurity and Data
Covers why cybersecurity matters and how organizations protect their most critical asset — data. I expanded on several topics here through additional research to build a more complete picture.

**Key topics covered:**
- Why cybersecurity is critical for every organization (financial, legal, reputational impact of breaches)
- Common threats: malware, phishing, ransomware, social engineering, DDoS, insider threats
- The three pillars of cybersecurity: **People + Processes + Technology**
- Data classification (Confidential, Private, Sensitive, Public) and data states (at rest, in transit, in use)
- Data protection methods: encryption, access controls, data masking, tokenization, backups
- Key privacy regulations: GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA, IT Act 2000
- GAPP — the 10 Generally Accepted Privacy Principles
- **The CIA Triad** — Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
- Security controls: Administrative, Physical, and Technical
- Data Loss Prevention (DLP) — how sensitive data is kept from leaving an organization
- Authentication methods and MFA

### Course 2 — Your Future in Cybersecurity: The Job Landscape
A career-focused course on the cybersecurity job market and roles. I went further into specific role tracks and skill-building paths based on my own research alongside the course material.

**Key topics covered:**
- Why cybersecurity is one of the most in-demand careers globally (millions of unfilled roles)
- Industries that hire cybersecurity professionals: banking, healthcare, government, energy, retail, and more
- **Red Team vs Blue Team** — offensive and defensive roles explained
- Key roles: SOC Analyst, Penetration Tester, Incident Responder, Threat Hunter, Cloud Security Engineer, GRC Analyst, CISO, and more
- Baseline skills every cybersecurity professional needs (networking, Linux, scripting, cloud basics)
- Workplace skills employers value: communication, report writing, attention to detail, ethical judgment
- Specialized skill tracks: SOC/Blue Team, Pen Testing/Red Team, Cloud Security, GRC/Compliance
- Certification roadmap: CompTIA Security+, CEH, eJPT, CySA+, OSCP, CISSP
- Free learning platforms: TryHackMe, Hack The Box, Cybrary, SANS Cyber Aces, IBM SkillsBuild
- Step-by-step guide to breaking into the field

### Course 3 — Protecting a Device with Malwarebytes
A hands-on course on device and network threats, with a practical simulation using the Malwarebytes suite. Notes here include deeper dives into topics like cookies, eavesdropping, and VPNs that I explored more thoroughly alongside the simulation work.

**Key topics covered:**
- Types of malware: viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, adware, rootkits, keyloggers, botnets
- How malware spreads and signs of infection
- Antimalware detection methods: signature-based, heuristic, real-time, sandboxing, quarantine
- Social engineering attacks: phishing, spear phishing, vishing, smishing, pretexting, baiting, tailgating
- Cookies — types, uses, and security risks (session hijacking, XSS, CSRF, cross-site tracking)
- Eavesdropping and network sniffing — passive, active, Man-in-the-Middle attacks
- VPNs — how they work, what they protect against, and their limitations
- **Hands-on Malwarebytes simulation:**
  - Scanning for malware and managing quarantine with **Malwarebytes**
  - Removing adware, spyware, and PUPs with **AdwCleaner**
  - Hiding device IP and encrypting traffic with **Privacy VPN**
  - Blocking online threats in the browser with **Browser Guard**

## 🔑 Key Concepts at a Glance

| Concept | One-line Summary |
|---------|-----------------|
| **CIA Triad** | Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability — the foundation of all security |
| **People + Process + Technology** | All three must work together; a gap in any one is a vulnerability |
| **Social Engineering** | Humans are easier to exploit than well-configured systems |
| **Defense in Depth** | Layer multiple controls — no single tool is enough |
| **Malwarebytes + AdwCleaner** | Together they cover system-level and browser-level threats |
| **VPN** | Encrypts traffic and hides IP — essential on public Wi-Fi |
| **Red vs Blue Team** | Offensive (attack/test) vs Defensive (monitor/protect) roles |
